# Project Governance

Razberi is committed to being an open, auditable, and community-driven project. Our governance model evolves with the project's maturity and funding status.

## 1. Current Phase: BDFL

During the initial research and development phase (active until the end of the **NLnet grant period**), the project operates under a **Benevolent Dictator For Life (BDFL)** model. 

* **Final Authority**: The Lead Developer (Project Founder) has final say on all architectural decisions, technical standards, and pull request approvals.
* **Objective**: To ensure rapid iteration and a consistent design vision during the pre-alpha and alpha stages.

## 2. Transition Plan: Steering Committee

We recognize that long-term sustainability requires a broader representation of stakeholders, especially from the legal and humanitarian sectors.

* **Transition Timeline**: Within **12 months of the v1.0 release**, Razberi will transition from the BDFL model to a multi-stakeholder **Steering Committee**.
* **Composition**: The Steering Committee will consist of at least three members, including:
    * **≥1 NGO Partner Representative**: A representative from a civil society organization or legal aid NGO working with refugees in Bulgaria.
    * Technical leads and core contributors.
* **Decision Making**: Post-transition, major strategic and technical decisions will be made via consensus among committee members.

## 3. Conflict Resolution

In the BDFL phase, the Project Founder resolves all conflicts. In the Steering Committee phase, a formal voting process will be defined as part of the transition charter.
